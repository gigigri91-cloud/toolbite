Frequently asked questions

What can I encode in a QR code?

URLs, plain text, email addresses, phone numbers, and Wi-Fi credentials. Any text up to a few thousand characters can be stored, though shorter content produces simpler, easier-to-scan codes.

Is this QR code generator free?

Yes — completely free, no account needed, no watermarks, no download limits. Generate and download as many PNG QR codes as you need.

What does the error correction level mean?

It determines how much of the QR code can be obscured while still scanning correctly. Low (L) gives a smaller, simpler code. High (H) adds redundancy — useful for print where a logo might overlap the code.

About QR Codes and Encoding

A QR code (Quick Response code) is a type of two-dimensional matrix barcode invented in 1994. Unlike standard linear barcodes, which store data in a single row of bars, QR codes store data vertically and horizontally, allowing them to hold significantly more information—up to 4,296 alphanumeric characters.

Because smartphones have built-in QR code scanners in their native camera apps, these codes have become the ubiquitous method for bridging offline physical media with online digital content. Whether printed on a restaurant menu, a business card, or a billboard, a simple scan instantly redirects the user to a target URL.

ToolBite's free QR Code Generator uses robust algorithms to map your input string into the black and white squares (modules) of the QR code. We expose adjustable error correction capabilities: higher error correction (Level H) means up to 30% of the QR code can be damaged, covered, or removed, and the data will still resolve perfectly.
